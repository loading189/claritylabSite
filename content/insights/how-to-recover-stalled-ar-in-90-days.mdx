If AR is drifting up, most owners react with pressure. More reminders. Sharper language. More urgency on every phone call.

That usually creates two problems: clients feel attacked, and your office burns time on emotional follow-up instead of clean execution. The fix is not aggression. The fix is cadence.

A 90-day AR recovery sprint should run like operations work: clear buckets, clear owners, clear next actions, same day every week.

## Week 1-2: Build the board and stop random follow-up

Start with one shared AR board. You can use a spreadsheet if needed. Columns should be invoice number, customer name, age bucket, amount, next action date, owner, and status.

Segment everything into 0-30, 31-60, and 61+ days. Do not skip this. If your team is working from a single long list, the oldest invoices disappear behind easier wins.

Set one 30-minute AR block every week. Same day, same time. This becomes non-negotiable. Random follow-up is what got you here.

## Week 3-6: Standardize communication before you escalate tone

Most delayed payments are not bad intent. They are approval gaps, PO mismatch, invoice not received, or a missing closeout detail from your side.

Use a simple communication ladder:

- Email reminder with invoice attached and due date confirmation.
- Phone follow-up 48 hours later if no response.
- Owner-level check-in for key accounts after 14 days in bucket.

Keep language practical: "Wanted to confirm this was received and ask when we should expect payment." Avoid threatening language until you have confirmed facts.

Create email templates and a call script so the tone stays consistent across the team. That protects relationships and keeps office staff from improvising under pressure.

## Week 7-10: Fix root causes behind new AR, not just old AR

If invoices keep entering AR late, collections work will always feel uphill.

Track three upstream metrics every week:

- Days from job completion to invoice sent
- % of invoices with missing documentation
- % of jobs closed same day in the system

Invoice lag usually creates most of the pain. If the field closes jobs late or closeout notes are incomplete, your office team cannot send clean invoices fast.

Set a same-day closeout standard for every completed job. Tie it to dispatcher and field lead accountability, not just accounting.

## Week 11-13: Lock the operating rhythm

By this stage, you should see fewer surprises and more predictable collections.

Now formalize the rhythm so AR does not drift again:

- Weekly AR review with office lead and owner (30 minutes)
- Monthly customer risk review for top 20 balances
- Clear policy for deposits and milestone billing on larger jobs
- Quarterly script refresh for call/email templates

Do not overbuild tooling. A simple board run consistently beats a complex system nobody updates.
